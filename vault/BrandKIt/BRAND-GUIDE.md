## Core idea

NOBS should feel friendly, modern, clean, and secure.

The identity is a simple wordmark plus one signal dot. It should read as:

- personal, not corporate
- modern, not noisy
- secure, not surveillance-coded
- friendly enough for families
- precise enough for Apple users

## Color anchors

- Ink: `#0A0D12`
- White: `#F7F9FC`
- Apple Blue: `#3478F6`
- Signal Green: `#30A46C`
- Amber: `#D98A18`
- Muted: `#66707C`
- Panel: `#171C25`
- Light: `#F7F8FB`

## Usage

- Use the wordmark for website headers, decks, screenshots, and product pages.
- Use the dot mark only when space is tight, such as favicons and avatars.
- Use the dark wordmark on light backgrounds and the light wordmark on dark backgrounds.
- Use banners as templates; keep the signal-dot identity and motion-line language.
- Do not use the old amber tile or shield identity for new images.

## Tone

Use short, direct lines. Keep copy human and grounded.

Good:

- `Private AI.`
- `Runs local.`
- `Private by default.`
- `Ask before acting.`
- `Your AI, on your side of the wall.`

Avoid:

- generic SaaS claims
- abstract futurist language
- glossy enterprise phrasing
- lock/shield clichés unless the context is explicitly security documentation
